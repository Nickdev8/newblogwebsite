## **What is Moonshot**
Moonshot is another HackClub event, where you make a project with 75+ hours.  
You get to go to Orlando, Florida, Universal Studios, and take a tour at Kennedy Space Center.

## **What is HackClub**
Hack Club is a non-profit that encourages teenagers to code.  
They host events where you get prizes for shipping personal projects.

## **We got what we need!**
For Moonshot, me and Ruben Straks made a project for school. A Disco Prullenbak (trash bin).  
It is a big blue bin where, as soon as you throw something in, music starts playing, LEDs start dancing, a smoke machine goes off, and a disco ball spins.

Along with some other projects, I got enough hours to come.

## **It’s official!**
I have got the tickets, post-event accommodation, hotels in Orlando and Miami, and a nice layover in New York during Christmas.  
This is going to be amazing. It still does not fully feel real.

## **Morning Chaos**

I woke up about **30 minutes** before I had to leave. No slow start. Just grabbing the last things, rushing out the door, and driving straight to Schiphol.

The flight was long. About 10 hours.
I passed the time by sleeping. Then sleeping some more. Somewhere in between, I watched the flight map crawl across the ocean and tried to make the hours disappear.

Honestly, it went by faster than expected.

## **We Landed**
After landing, we looked around for the adult picking us up. That was **Jared**. We talked for a bit until **Moukal** arrived, and then the four of us took an Uber to the hotel.

![](selfie met nas en jarrod)

## **Food Before Anything Else**
First stop after arriving was the dining hall.
I was not even that hungry, but somehow still ended up with way too much food. Travel does that. You tell yourself you are just going to grab something small, and ten minutes later there is cake involved.

It was impressive.

## **Opening Ceremony**
After checking in and feeling extremely tired, we went straight back down for the opening ceremony. This is where they explained the rules, the schedule, and what the next few days would look like.

One rule that will be important for tomorrow's blog. We are not allowed to leave the hotel or venue.

## The Project Prompt
That night, our team picked three notes. Each note had one word on it. Together, they formed the theme for our project.

We got:
* *Cryptic message*
* *Fail*
* *Overheated laptop*

## The Idea
We decided to make a word game. You unscramble words and sentences.
Every time you lose a life, your score multiplier goes up. So the whole game becomes a balance between playing it safe or taking risks.

**Not bad for day one.**

title: "Orlando Day 2: A Misunderstanding"
date: "2025-12-13"
---

## **Morning**
I woke up around 10am, with Universal Studios planned for later that day. For lunch, we were given $20 and some free time before leaving.

With a few hours to spare, I thought it would be fine to quickly grab food off-site.

That assumption turned out to be wrong.

## **What Went Wrong**
I misunderstood the boundaries around leaving the venue. Even though waivers allowed unsupervised time during specific activities, I overlooked the rule that we were not allowed to leave the hotel outside of those moments.

That part is on me.

## **Back at the Hotel**
I returned as soon as I was asked to and spoke with the organizers. Because I had left the venue, I was told I could not join the Universal Studios outing and needed to stay in my room and remain reachable.

What bothered me was not just the consequence, but how it felt in the moment.

## **Where the Frustration Set In**
What made it hard was that the response felt focused on me personally. I was told I needed to be more careful about following the rules. I was told that if it were Graham’s decision alone, I would have been sent home that same day.

That hit **hard.**

In that moment it stopped feeling like a mistake and started feeling personal.

## **How It Felt**
I could not shake the feeling that this might not have been just about that day. At the time I wondered whether my past issues within Hack Club played a role in how quickly things escalated.

I owned the mistake. But in that moment, being the focus of the conversation felt unfair to me.

## **Reflection**
Rules matter. I understand that.
But so does fairness.

This day left me frustrated, confused, and honestly pretty hurt.
Not because of Universal Studios.
But because of how fast trust seemed to disappear.

Lesson learned.

title: "New York: One Long Day"
date: "2025-12-24T12:00:00"

## **Thrown Into It**
I landed in New York after a short flight from Miami, running on almost no sleep. The difference hit instantly. Cold air. Faster pace. No soft landing.

![](/blogimages/moonshot/24decairplanetojfk.JPG)

From the airport it was straight into movement. Trains, transfers, walking, following signs, dragging bags. I was awake, but barely present.

![](/blogimages/moonshot/24inmetrojfk.JPG)

## **Accidental Landmarks**
As soon as I exited the metro, I accidentally walked straight into **Rockefeller Center**.

![](/blogimages/moonshot/newyork.png)

Crowds everywhere. People stacked on top of each other. Lights, noise, cameras, the Christmas tree. Impressive, yes. But overwhelming. Way too much as a first impression.

I dropped off my luggage as quickly as possible, which helped immediately. No bags made the city feel lighter.

Looking for something quieter, I walked into a side street.

Which turned out to be **Times Square**.

Again. Screens, lights, crowds, chaos. Tourists stopping randomly, people rushing past them. Everything screaming for attention.

*Not again.*

So I walked away, following smaller streets, just trying to escape the noise.

And somehow, without planning it at all, I ended up on **Broadway**. Right in front of the theater where **Hamilton** takes place. That moment felt different. Still busy, but calmer. Purposeful. Genuinely cool.

## **Reset Mode**
After all that stimulus, I stopped at a **McDonald’s**. Nothing special. Just warmth, food, and somewhere to sit.

Exhaustion caught up fast, so I went to a movie theater just to rest. Darkness, seats, quiet. Exactly what I needed.

![](/blogimages/moonshot/24lastdayaftermovie.JPG)

## **Cold Streets and a Closed City**
After the movie, I went back outside. It was colder. I walked south for a bit to see the **Empire State Building**, but the cold kept creeping in.

What surprised me most was how dead the city felt. Christmas Eve. Almost everything closed. Few places to warm up. Few places to just exist.

After more than two hours of walking, the only place I found open was an **Apple Store**. I went in and sat down on a bench.

Apparently, I fell asleep almost instantly. At some point, a worker came up and told me I could not sleep there. Fair enough. I was completely gone by then.

## **The Scam**
Eventually, I woke up properly and booked a sleeping pod near Central Park for about 200 bucks. It was around 4am when I arrived.

There was a guy there. Let’s call him *Jamal*.

He asked for copies of my ID, a signature, and a 50-dollar deposit. Then he told me I had to wait until 5am to check in.

When I finally got the room, something immediately felt wrong. It was not what I expected, I was exhausted, and the whole setup felt sketchy.

Then things escalated. Jamal became pushy, told me I had to leave, and refused to give a refund. I had not even been there for ten minutes.

While standing awkwardly in the elevator, I got an email.  
A **$250 charge** had just been made to my card.

In total, I was down almost **$500**, without a place to sleep.

Later, I would see Jamal laying in a subway hallway. Homeless. Scamming people. That made everything click. Anger, frustration, disbelief. I was tired, cold, and taken advantage of.

## **Just Sit Somewhere**
By then it was almost 6am. At 7am, I booked a seat at a cinema just for somewhere warm to sit.

I watched **Zootopia 2**, but mostly I slept. I arrived hours early, sat down, and passed out until loud moments in the movie woke me up.

## **One Last Thing**
I felt like I had seen enough of New York. Except one thing.

I took the subway to **World Trade Center** and visited the **9/11 Memorial**. Quiet. Heavy. Beautiful in a respectful way.

After that, I walked to **Battery Park** and took the **Staten Island Ferry**. The Statue of Liberty was smaller than expected. Still special. Still worth seeing.

## **Leaving**
And just like that, it was time to go.

New York was not easy. Not gentle. Not warm.

But it was *real.*

And somehow, that made it unforgettable.

## **The End of This Chapter**
This trip has been the best vacation I have ever had.

It was full of laughter, stress, strange moments, beautiful places, and memories I will not forget. I saw incredible things. I had calm days on the beach. I had chaotic nights in New York. I had moments where everything felt easy, and moments where I just wanted to sit down somewhere warm and disappear for a bit.

But that is what made it feel real.

There was always something next. Another bus. Another beach. Another meal. Another mistake. Another weird American store. Another place that looked completely different from the last one.

## **What I Miss Already**
I want to go back.

Back to South Beach. Back to the sand. Back to the warmth. Back to waking up with no real plan except finding something to do and seeing where the day goes.

That is what I miss most.
Not one big moment.
Just the feeling of being there.

## **For Future Me**
If I ever read this again, I hope I remember the whole thing honestly.

Not just the pretty parts.
Also the tired parts. The confusing parts. The parts where I messed up. The parts where the city felt too loud, the bus felt unsafe, or the day went completely sideways.

Because somehow, all of that became the story.

And if I ever get days like these again, you can bet I will be blogging it again.

## **Thank You**
Thank you to everyone who followed along, supported me, and made this trip possible.

This was more than a vacation.  
It was a memory I will carry with me forever.
